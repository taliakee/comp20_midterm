Success Spotlight: Bella

In 2017, Bella was found under a bridge covered in sewer water in Boston, MA. When we found her, she was covered in bruises, scratch marks and had swollen ankles. Bella fell victim to domestic abuse and showed signs of malnourishment. She had a long journey ahead for her physical and mental recovery.

One Friday afternoon, the O'Reilly family visited our shelter, hoping to rescue a young/middle-aged dog. They met Bella and instantly fell in love with her sweet face and bubbly personality. At that point, Bella had completed her three months of therapy and was ready to join her new family.

Since that Friday afternoon, Bella has been a lovely addition to the O'Reilley family for the past three years. She loves playing in the New England snow and snuggles with the kids during their weekly Harry Potter movie marathons. Bella is happy, healthy, and safe â€” everything we could hope for.

Why Rescue?

Each year over 600,000 shelter dogs are euthanized. While lack of owner interest is the main reason dogs grow old
in these shelters, insufficient resources such as food, water, space, and sanitation are other factors. Shelter dogs,
oftentimes having been abandoned, deserve the utmost love and attention. We serve to provide new homes and
happier lives for these pups.
